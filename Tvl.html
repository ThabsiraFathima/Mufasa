Tirunelveli


Famous food in Tirunelveli

Halwa is a popular, dense, sweet confection enjoyed across many cultures, with roots in the Middle East.
The name "halwa" itself comes from the Arabic word "hulw," meaning sweet.
It's known for its diverse regional variations and preparation methods, often involving ingredients like flour, sugar, and clarified butter (ghee).

Here are some interesting facts about halwa:
Origin:
 While the exact origin is debated, it's widely believed that halwa originated in the Middle East and spread to India via Persia.
Variations:
 Halwa is known for its numerous regional variations, each with unique flavors and ingredients. Some popular types include sooji (semolina) halwa, gajar (carrot) ka halwa, and atta (wheat flour) halwa.
